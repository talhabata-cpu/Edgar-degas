Edgar Degas

Research on Artwork: The Ballet Class

01

The Artist

French Impressionist known for his revolutionary approach to capturing movement and everyday subjects.

  • Born 1834, Paris — from aristocratic background
  • Originally trained in classical academic painting
  • Rejected traditional Salon exhibition in 1874
  • Became founding member of Impressionist movement

02

The Artwork

La Classe de Danse (The Ballet Class), 1874

Oil on canvas, 85 x 75 cm. Held at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York.

One of Degas's most celebrated depictions of ballet life behind the scenes.

03

Formal Analysis

The "Keyhole" Perspective

  • Viewer positioned as unseen observer from behind
  • Creates sense of spying on private moment
  • Dancer positioned centrally — focal point
  • Off-center composition challenges tradition

* The unusual vantage point draws viewers into the rehearsal space.

04

Medium & Technique

Oil paint applied with remarkable spontaneity to mimic rapid sketches.

  • Visible brushstrokes suggest movement and energy
  • Loose handling in background contrasts with detailed foreground
  • Influenced by photography — candid, unplanned feel
  • Blurred edges create sense of passing time

05

The Subject

Ballet Rehearsals — The Unseen Reality

  • Degas fascinated by discipline and labor of dancers
  • Shows exhaustion, not glamour
  • Exposes the rigorous training behind performance
  • Championed "ordinary" subjects in fine art

* Unlike romanticized portrayals, Degas revealed the athlete's dedication.

06

Personal Interpretation

Emotional Response

  • The dim lighting evokes quiet contemplation
  • Subtle melancholy beneath apparent industriousness
  • I sense respect for these young artists' commitment
  • The painting invites reflection on unseen effort

07

Conclusion

Degas's Enduring Legacy

  • Pioneered observation of modern urban life
  • Influenced 20th-century photography and cinema
  • Changed how motion could be depicted in painting
  • His honest approach inspired countless artists

Edgar Degas transformed art by finding beauty in everyday dedication.
